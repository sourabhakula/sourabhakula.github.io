My Approach

How I work through a problem

Four things I come back to on every project, regardless of the tool or the domain.

01

Listen before querying

I start every project by sitting with the people who will use the work. What are they actually trying to decide? What keeps them up at night? The best analysis starts with understanding the real question, not just the one on the ticket.

02

Chase what feels off

When something in the data doesn't match what everyone assumes, that's where I dig in. The most useful findings I've delivered came from noticing a number that didn't sit right and following it until I understood why.

03

Build it so it lasts

I don't like building something once and throwing it away. Whether it's a dbt pipeline or a Power BI dashboard, I think about who's going to maintain this after me. Clean models, tested logic, clear documentation.

04

Close the loop

Analysis that sits in a notebook doesn't change anything. I write memos, present findings, and follow up to see if the recommendation actually worked. The job isn't done when the query runs. It's done when someone acts on it.
